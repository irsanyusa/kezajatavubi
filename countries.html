Chilean History Timeline - Important Dates &amp; Events

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, July 7, 2024

Events in Chilean History

  • 1541-02-12 Santiago, Chile, founded (or Feb 24)
  • 1541-02-24 Santiago, Chile founded by Pedro de Valvidia (or 2/12)
  • 1541-09-11 Santiago, Chile, is destroyed by indigenous warriors, lead by Michimalonko
  • 1550-10-05 Foundation of the city of Concepción, Chile.
  • 1553-12-25 Battle of Tucapel: Mapuche rebels under Lautaro defeats the Spanish conquistadors and executes the governor of Chile Pedro de Valdivia
  • 1574-11-22 Discovery of uninhabited Juan Fernández Islands off Chile by Spanish sailor Juan Fernández - later famous home of marooned sailor Alexander Selkirk (inspired Robinson Crusoe story)

The Golden Hind

1578-08-20 Francis Drake renames his flagship the 'Pelican' to the 'Golden Hind' in honor of his patron, Sir Christopher Hatton [1]

  • 1598-12-21 Battle of Curalaba: the Mapuche people led by Pelentaru revolt and inflict a major defeat on Spanish troops in southern Chile
  • 1643-05-13 Heavy earthquake strikes Santiago, Chile; kills a third of the population
  • 1810-09-18 Chile declares independence from Spain (National Day)

San Martin Crosses the Andres

1817-01-18 Argentine general José de San Martín leads a revolutionary army over the Andes to attack Spanish royalists in Chile

  • 1834-11-21 HMS Beagle anchors at Bay of San Carlos, Chile
  • 1835-02-20 Concepcion, Chile, destroyed by earthquake; 5,000 die
  • 1835-03-11 HMS Beagle anchors off Valparaiso, Chile
  • 1835-05-12 Charles Darwin visits Alexander Caldcleugh’s copper mines at Panuncillo in northern Chile and is impressed by the extraordinary feats of labor performed by the workers
  • 1836-11-11 Chile declares war on Bolivia & Peru
  • 1839-01-20 Battle of Yungay, Chile defeats a Peruvian and Bolivian alliance
  • 1863-12-08 2,500 reported killed as result of fire at Jesuit Church of La Compana, Santiago, Chile
  • 1865-11-26 Battle of Papudo: The Spanish navy engages a combined Peruvian-Chilean fleet north of Valparaiso, Chile
  • 1865-12-05 Chincha Islands War: Peru allies with Chile against Spain.
  • 1866-03-31 The Spanish navy bombards the harbor of Valparaíso, Chile
  • 1868-08-08 Earthquake destroys the city of Arica, Chile
  • 1879-02-14 Chilean troops occupy Antofagasta
  • 1879-03-23 War of the Pacific fought between Chile and the joints forces of Bolivia and Peru. Chile successfully takes over Arica and Tarapacá, leaving Bolivia a landlocked country.
  • 1879-04-05 Chile declares war on Bolivia and Peru, starting the War of the Pacific.
  • 1879-05-21 Battle of Iquique: Chilean naval forces overcome Peruvian ships (War of the Pacific)
  • 1879-10-08 War of the Pacific: the Chilean Navy defeats the Peruvian Navy in the Battle of Angamos, Peruvian Admiral Miguel Grau is killed in the encounter.
  • 1883-10-20 Treaty of Ancon; Peru cedes Tarapaca to Chile
  • 1904-03-13 Bronze statue of Christ on Argentine-Chilean border dedicated
  • 1904-10-20 Bolivia and Chile sign a treaty ending the War of the Pacific; recognizing Chile's possession of the coast, providing for construction of a railway linking La Paz, Bolivia, to Arica on the coast
  • 1906-08-16 -17] 8.6 earthquake destroys Valparaiso Chile, fire kills 20,000

Wilson Accepts Mediation

1914-04-25 US President Woodrow Wilson is persuaded by Argentina, Brazil, and Chile to accept mediation in the conflict with Mexico

  • 1914-11-01 German-British naval battle at Coronel, Chile
  • 1915-03-14 German cruiser Dresden scuttled off Más a Tierra, Chile, having been pursued by the Royal Navy after the Battle of the Falkland Islands, with her engines worn out and virtually no coal
  • 1927-04-11 Chilean general Carlos Ibáñez names himself president
  • 1927-04-27 Carabineros de Chile (Chilean national police force and gendarmery) created
  • 1929-06-03 Chile and Peru sign the Treaty of Lima, finally resolving their border dispute from the War of the Pacific (1879–83). Chile keeps Arica and Peru regains Tacna.
  • 1931-07-27 Chilean president Carlos Ibáñez forced out
  • 1931-10-04 Juan Esteban Montero becomes President of Chile
  • 1932-06-04 Chilean coup led by Colonel Marmaduke Grove against President Juan Esteban Montero
  • 1932-09-14 Military coup in Chile under Arturo Alessandri
  • 1932-12-24 Arturo Alessandri wins presidential election in Chile
  • 1939-01-24 30,000 killed by earthquake in Concepcion Chile
  • 1939-01-25 Earthquake hits Chillan Chile, 10,000 killed
  • 1939-01-30 Heavy aftershocks destroy some of Chile
  • 1943-01-31 Chile breaks off relations with Germany & Japan
  • 1945-02-14 Peru, Paraguay, Chile & Ecuador joins UN
  • 1947-10-11 Brazil & Chile break diplomatic relations with USSR
  • 1952-09-05 General Carlos Ibáñez elected president of Chile
  • 1953-05-07 Record 537-kg swordfish is caught by LE Marron, in Chile
  • 1955-06-14 Chile becomes a signatory to the Buenos Aires copyright treaty
  • 1962-06-17 FIFA World Cup Final, Estadio Nacional, Santiago, Chile: Brazil go 1-down early but recover to beat Czechoslovakia, 3-1
  • 1964-09-04 Eduardo Frei Montalva elected President of Chile
  • 1965-03-07 Christian-democrats win parliament in Chile
  • 1969-07-10 Chilean Association of Librarians created
  • 1970-06-05 Chile becomes a member of the Berne Convention copyright treaty
  • 1970-10-24 Marxist Salvador Allende elected President of Chile by the Chilean Congress
  • 1970-11-03 Marxist Salvador Allende inaugurated as President of Chile
  • 1970-12-31 President Allende nationalizes Chilean coal mines
  • 1971-03-29 Chile president Allende nationalizes banks/copper mines
  • 1971-07-11 Chilean parliament nationalizes American copper mines
  • 1972-06-17 Chilean President Salvador Allende forms a new socialist government
  • 1972-10-13 Uruguay to Chile plane crashes in Andes Mountains, (passengers eat crash victims to survive, 16 of 45 rescued 2 months later)
  • 1973-04-02 ITT admits to asking CIA to influence Chilean presidential election
  • 1973-08-22 Chilean parliament accuses pres Allende violating laws
  • 1973-09-11 Chilean President Salvador Allende, the 1st elected Marxist president of a South America country, is deposed in a military coup led by general Augusto Pinochet
  • 1973-09-28 ITT Building in New York City bombed to protest ITT's involvement in the September 11 1973 coup d'état in Chile.
  • 1976-09-21 Orlando Letelier is assassinated in Washington, D.C. He was a member of the Chilean socialist government of Salvador Allende, overthrown in 1973 by Augusto Pinochet.
  • 1976-12-18 Soviet dissident Viktor Bukovskiexchanged for Chile CP-leader Corvalan
  • 1976-12-19 Davis Cup Men's Tennis, Santiago, Chile: Paolo Bertolucci & Adriano Panatta give Italy unassailable 3-0 lead; beat Patricio Cornejo & Jaime Fillol of Chile 6-3, 2-6, 9-7, 6-3; ends, 4-1
  • 1977-03-12 Chilean dictator Augusto Pinochet bans the Christian-Democratic Party
  • 1979-01-08 Argentina & Chile sign Beagle Canal accord
  • 1979-11-08 The Chilean Communist Party (Proletarian Action) is formed.
  • 1980-09-11 Chile adopts its constitution
  • 1981-03-11 Chile constitution takes effect, Augusto Pinochet's second term begins
  • 1981-04-22 10,000 copper workers in Chile strike
  • 1986-07-02 General strike against Pinochet regime in Chile
  • 1986-09-07 Failed assassination attempt on Chilean dictator Augusto Pinochet, kills 5 people
  • 1987-05-26 Cecilia Bolocco, 22, of Chile, crowned 36th Miss Universe
  • 1987-10-03 Michael Pruffer of France skis 135.26 MPH at Portillo, Chile
  • 1988-10-05 Chile votes in a referendum 56-44 against extending Augusto Pinochet's regime by 8 years thus ending the dictator's 16½ years in power
  • 1989-03-12 2 cyanide-contaminated Chilean grapes found (Philadelphia)
  • 1989-03-13 FDA orders recall of all Chilean fruit in US
  • 1989-06-30 NASA closes down tracking stations in Santiago, Chile & Guam
  • 1989-07-30 Chile amends its constitution
  • 1991-06-18 Mud storm in Antofagasta Chile, kills 80
  • 1993-12-11 Eduardo Frei Ruiz-Tagle elected President of Chile
  • 1994-03-11 Eduardo Frei succeeds Patricio Aylwin as President of Chile
  • 1998-10-16 Former Chilean dictator General Augusto Pinochet is arrested in London on a Spanish warrant requesting his extradition on murder charges
  • 2000-03-02 Former Chilean dictator General Augusto Pinochet heads home after being told the UK would not extradite him on torture charges
  • 2004-08-21 Nicolás Massú & Fernando González win the men's doubles tennis at the Athens Olympics beating German pair Nicolas Kiefer & Rainer Schüttler in 5 sets; it's Chile's first Olympic gold medal
  • 2004-08-22 After winning the doubles tennis gold medal at the Athens Olympics, Chile's Nicolás Massú wins the men's singles gold 6–3, 3–6, 2–6, 6–3, 6–4 over American Mardy Fish
  • 2004-12-13 Former Chilean dictator, General Augusto Pinochet is put under house arrest, after being sued under accusations over 9 kidnapping actions and manslaughter. The house arrest is lifted the same day on appeal.
  • 2008-05-06 Chaiten Volcano erupts in Chile, forcing the evacuation of more than 4,500 people.

Nadal Olympic Champion

2008-08-17 Rafael Nadal of Spain beats Chile's Fernando González 6-3, 7-6, 6-3 to win the men's tennis singles at the Beijing Olympics; Spain's first ever Olympic gold medal in tennis

  • 2009-06-05 Chile officially enters recession; it is the first South American country to enter the global recession
  • 2010-02-27 8.8 magnitude earthquake rattles central Chile; epicenter just off the coast also triggers a tsunami

Copiapó Mine Rescue

2010-10-13 Copiapó mining accident in Chile comes to a happy end as all 33 miners arrive at the surface after surviving a record 69 days underground

  • 2015-09-16 8.3 magnitude quake hits off coast of Illapel, Chile killing 11 and prompting evacuation of 1 million
  • 2017-01-22 Chile declares a State of Emergency and requests international assistance as wildfires rage out of control
  • 2017-03-26 Mass protests in Chile over country's privatised pension system
  • 2017-07-02 FIFA Confederations Cup: Germany defeats Chile 1-0 to win for the 1st time at Krestovsky Stadium in Saint Petersburg, Russia
  • 2017-08-21 Chile's constitutional court approves bill to ease country's total abortion ban
  • 2017-08-23 World's driest place, the Atacama desert in Chile blooms after unexpected rainfall
  • 2017-12-17 Sebastián Piñera wins Chile's presidential election run-off, returning to office after 4 years

Pope Visits Chile

2018-01-15 Pope Francis arrives in Chile for a three-day visit amid child sexual abuse claims against church clergy

  • 2018-03-08 Asia-Pacific trade deal signed in Santiago, Chile by 11 countries after US pulled out
  • 2018-05-18 All of Chile's 34 Roman Catholic bishops offer their resignation to Pope Francis in wake of child sex scandal

Gravitational Redshift

2018-07-26 Observation of a black hole by The Very Large Telescope in Chile proves Albert Einstein's prediction of "gravitational redshift", published in "Journal Astronomy & Astrophysics"
