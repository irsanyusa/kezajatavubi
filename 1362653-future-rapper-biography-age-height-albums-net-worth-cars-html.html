At a young age, he started performing as a part of the musical group "The Dungeon Family". There, he was given his current stage name and was motivated by his cousin Rico Wade to continue his rapping career.

One of his first features was in "Racks", a collaboration with YC. Future gained popularity after his songs were played by DJ Esco at Magic City, a strip club in Atlanta

Future albums

Before releasing his debut album "Pluto" in 2012, the musician had produced a few mixtapes. "Pluto" contained a few collaborations with musicians like T.I, Drake, R.Kelly, Snoop Dogg, and Trae Tha Truth.

The following year, he also collaborated with artists such as Diddy, Ludacris, Kelly Rowland, and Rihanna. In 2013, his next album "Honest" came out.

Two years later, he put out his next album "DS2" and collaborated with Drake for a mixtape "What a Time to Be Alive". The rapper's fourth album "EVOL" premiered in 2016.

2017 was the year when the musician released a self-titled fifth album. A week later, he surprisingly made his sixth album "Hndrxx" public.

Along with fellow musicians, such as Kendrick Lamar, Jay Rock, and James Blake, he worked on the soundtrack for "Black Panther" titled "King's Dead", which received two Grammy nominations.

The seventh album "Future Hndrxx Presents: The Wizrd" came out in 2019 and was praised by critics. The same year, he received his first Grammy award for "King's Dead".

2019 was also when he released his first solo EP titled "Save Me". He also collaborated with Drake on two songs at the beginning of the next year.

In 2020, the latest Future album to date "High Off Life" came out, and it debuted at number one on Billboard 200. It became the musician's seventh album to top this chart.
